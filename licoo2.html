CoO2 

a cornucopia of wonders

Electron gas in 1d and 0d case is much much smaller than in 2deg case (around 50-60 times smaller.) this is an indication that Li-Li bond seems to be an important factor. In 1d case, since we put Li symmetrically on top and bottom, we only have 2 Li nearest neighbor and thus the density seems to be too small to form Li-Li states and this happens even in the 0D case. 

There are couple of other interesting things I did too. There are two possibilities that can actually explain this, we have been thinking ( or atleast I have) that Li wants to form Li-Li bond, but instead, what if CoO2 docent want one full e- and is happy just with 75% of what it gets ? So I did tests with LiNiO2 and LiFeO2 monolayers where Ni already has one extra e- and Fe need one more e- (if the physics is same) So If Li wants to form Li Li bond and if thats whats stabilizing the bond, then be it Ni or Fe the Li Li bond should form and have almost the same e- but thats not what I observe, there is a significant difference in LiFeO2 and LiNiO2 and LiCoO2 monolayer's electron gas's total free electron. I need to analysis the data more, but the electron density tends to depend on 
1) Dimensionality ( this makes sense in the LI-Li picture as in 1D case Li has just 2 Nearest neighbors and 0D no nearest neighbor (farther than 1D and 2D case) )
2) Li-CoO2, Li-NiO2, Li-FeO2 have all different electron density ( need to make sense of it)
3) K-CoO2 e- density is different than Li-CoO2 ( this again makes sense as K-K nearest bond in CoO2 shell is much stronger than Li-Li because of size, again confirming K-K interaction is whats being favored)
