CoO2 

a cornucopia of wonders

LiCoO2 is a layered quantum material which has been used for decades in batteries as electrode material. Our experimental and material scientists have been trying to synthesis monolayer CoO2 from this bulk material LiCoO2. This is really interesting system because, when Li is taken off CoO2, the CoO2 system looses an electron because of which many exciting things can happen in the quantum mechanical configuration of the Co atom's d-shell. Also adding to the fact is that the CoO2 layer in LiCoO2 has Co placed in triangular lattice, which would yet again create unique spin properties due to the possibility of spin frustration arising incase of Anti-Ferromagnetic interaction. This may also possibly be a very viable place to explore the possibility of the elusive spin-liquid state of quantum matter as all the key ingredients needed for it resides in the Monolayer CoO2 system.


LiCoO2 in its bulk form is a semiconductor with a band gap of around 1.8 eV. When one moves from bulk to 2 dimensional monolayer, physics seldom changes drastically (although there are cases where things do change). Since our experimental colleagues are trying to syntheses monolayer CoO2 from LiCoO2, our interest turned to look at LiCoO2 monolayer too. Things get interesting as monolayer LiCoO2 turns out to be a half metal with 2 dimensional electron gas like state forming above Li. This is interesting as one would not expect any drastic change in the quantum state of monolayer LiCoO2 when compared to its bulk counterpart and would expect it to be a semiconductor too.   

This video shows the quantum band structure of LiCoO2 monolayer when Li is moved away from the CoO2 layer. One can see the Nearly free electron like bands forming more rigidly as the Li is seperated from the CoO2. This shows that the 2 dimensional electron gas like band is actually formed by Li-Li interaction in its elemental state.
