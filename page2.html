Research & Projects

mage and parallax effect

My PhD is mainly focused on working with a new phase of matter called Topological quantum systems. These are quantum systems(mostly materials), where interestingly, new particles that are analogous to fundamental particles emerge from collective excitations of electrons in low energy. These kind of "quasi-particles" give rise to intriguing effects in real materials. Other than studying these low energy excitations, my work also focuses on trying to describe systems where one cannot think of electrons as individual non-interacting electrons anymore. These systems are the so called strongly correlated systems. These kind of systems where our notion of Independent electron picture breaks down, has exotic phases that pop up as emergent phenomena like High temperature superconductivity, Spin Density waves etc.. 

Though my main PhD work is in theoretical and computational physics, my interests range from mathematical modeling of carnatic music to deep-learning. I am excited about anything that involves mathematics and dabble in them. 

Feynman used to say that there were two kinds of physicists, the Babylonians and the Greeks. The Babylonians made civilization's first great strides in understanding numbers and equations, but it was the Greeks whom we credit with inventing mathematics. Put simply, the Babylonians focused on the phenomenon, the Greeks on the underlying order.Feynman considered himself a Babylonian; Gell-Mann, Feynman's contemporary, was more the Greek type, wanting to categorize nature and impose an efficient mathematical order on the underlying data. I would classify myself as the latter, the Greek. I am fascinated by the language of mathematics to describe, in most elegant way, the properties and rules of nature.

Theoretical Physics

My journey in physics is very similar to a kid in candy store, initial starting my research in gradational theory and slowly meandering the exciting world of condensed matter physics. My early work involved studying the mathematical framework of classical (non-relativistic) gravitational fields and the underdetermination of classical gradational theories. I latter came across (and got attracted to) Condensed matter theory and quantum many-body physics which I am currently working now. Apart from these, I also work on using first-principle computational physics to design and understand quantum systems for various applications including solar cells, ferroelectrics, electro-chromic materials, topological insulators, magnetic semiconductors and battery technology.

Ladder Diagrams via Bootstrap kernal : In quantum systems where electron-electron interactions are extremely high (Correlated systems), one needs to go beyond the simple first order description in many-body theory and add higher order diagrams to get any tractable result. My current research involves computing ladder diagrams in Hedien's equation via bootstrap kernel as an alternate to Bethe-Salpeter equation

Classical Gravity as a curvature in pure Space manifold : This research involved explaining the necessity of a time-like dimension even in the most simple case of non-relativistic theory, without which one cannot write consistent Lagrangian for gravitational fields.

Essence of Quantum Many-Body-Perturbation theory : This review article was written to derive the ubiquitous Hedin and GW equations purely using Feynman diagrams starting from field theoretic description of many-body system. 

Theory of Halide perovskites : Perovskites rich in had the in a special class of inorganic which a promising for solar cells. Are rich in different phases, we had studied the different distortion in a special class of inorganic halide perovskites which are a very promising candidate for perovskite solar cells.

Ferro-electric ordering in Heisenberg Hameltonian : We have developed a simple explanation of the existence of a completely different phase of the inorganic halide perovskites by mapping them to a transverse field spin Heisenberg Hamiltonian in a classical regime.

Honeycomb Sb (Topological Insulator) : Monolayer Sb usually exists in phosphorus like buckled layer which flattens out with the of Hydrogen on both the . This flat honeycomb version of Sb has unique quadruple Dirac points because of both symmetry (like graphene) and orbital energies. We are studying the different symmetries and properties of these Dirac cones.

CoO2 Monolayers : We are currently collaborating with experimentalists to synthesise and explore a new quantum 2-dimensional material CoO2 from LiCoO2. We have discovered that this material is a ferromagnetic semiconductor, which is an extremely rare breed with myriad uses. It also shows early signs of being a kondo insulator.
